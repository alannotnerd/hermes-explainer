Risk Analysis

Detection Engine — What Signal Looks For

Seven signals are analyzed per token launch to identify suspicious patterns before they reach exchanges.

🔗 Wallet Coordination
Clusters of wallets acting in concert to simulate organic activity
♻️ Recycled Wallets
Addresses reused across multiple suspicious launches
📉 Float Auth
Fake liquidity or artificially inflated circulating float
🎯 Concentrated Exposure
Tokens concentrated in a small number of hands, creating manipulation risk
⚠️ Abnormal Claims
Unusual claim patterns that deviate from stated distribution rules
📭 Low Protocol Usage
Token with little to no genuine on-chain activity or protocol interaction
🔬 Behavioral Anomalies
Deviations from normal launch patterns based on historical baselines

🔍 Detection engine: 7 signals analyzed per token launch

Process

Workflow — How a Review Works

From data submission to risk alert — the end-to-end review pipeline for every token launch.

STEP 1
📤 Submit
Token data is submitted for review, triggering the onboarding pipeline
STEP 2
👁 Monitor
On-chain monitoring by 3rd-party vendor, tracking wallets and transactions
STEP 3
🔎 Review
Analysts review flagged patterns against the detection engine signals
STEP 4
🚨 Alert
Risk alerts with conditions are generated and distributed to partners

🔄 Alerts are continuously enriched by the 3rd-party monitoring vendor via webhook callbacks to Signal's endpoint.

Engineering

Technical Challenges

The hardest problems to solve in building a reliable pre-listing risk intelligence platform.

CHALLENGE 1
Webhook Reliability
Ensure the webhook endpoint can handle vendor payloads reliably — idempotency, retries, schema validation, and alert deduplication before surfacing to the dashboard.
CHALLENGE 2
State Changes Over Time
Wallet behavior evolves — an address that looks clean during launch may participate in manipulation later. Tracking these state transitions without false positives is non-trivial.
CHALLENGE 3
Historical Baseline
Building a reliable historical baseline to distinguish normal from anomalous. Without history, every pattern looks suspicious — or nothing does. Requires months of labeled data.
